To apply the rule of thirds:

  1. Visualize the grid or activate it in your camera settings. Most digital cameras and smartphones have a grid overlay option.
  2. Position key elements along the grid lines or at the intersections. For landscapes, try aligning the horizon with the top or bottom horizontal line rather than the middle.
  3. For portraits, place the subject's eyes at or near the top intersection points.
  4. Experiment with different placements to find the most appealing composition.
{% elif lesson_id == 4 %}

While learning to use the rule of thirds, avoid these common mistakes:

  1. Centering everything - sometimes this works, but often creates static, uninteresting images.
  2. Ignoring the background - consider how all elements interact with the grid.
  3. Not considering the horizon line - avoid placing it in the middle of the frame.
  4. Being too strict with the rules - remember that the rule of thirds is a guideline, not a law. Once you understand when to use it, you'll also learn when to break it for creative effect.
